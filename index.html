Colour

The property possessed by an object of producing different sensations on the eye as a result of the way it reflects or emits light.

Primary Colours

Any of a group of colours from which all other colours can be obtained by mixing.

RGB (red, green, and blue), CMY (cyan, magenta, and yellow), and RYB (red, yellow, and blue)

  • Red
  • Green
  • Blu
  • CMY
  • RGB

Secondary Colors

These are color combinations created by the equal mixture of two primary colors. On the color wheel, secondary colors are located between primary colors.

According to the traditional color wheel, red and yellow make orange, red and blue make purple, and blue and yellow make green.

Black

Lacking hue and brightness; absorbing light without reflecting any of the rays composing it.

Black RGB color code = #000000= 0*65536+0*256+0 = (0,0,0) RED=0, GREEN=0, BLUE=0

White

Pigments that contribute light-scattering properties to coatings are generally known as white, or hiding, pigments.

The hexadecimal color #FFFFFF, known as white, has RGB values of R:255 G:255, B:255 and CMYK values of C:0, M:0, Y:0, K:0.
